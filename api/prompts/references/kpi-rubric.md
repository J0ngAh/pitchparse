# Sales Call KPI Rubric — Detailed Scoring Guide

## 1. Talk-to-Listen Ratio

**What to measure:** Percentage of total words spoken by the consultant vs the prospect.

| Score | Criteria |
|-------|----------|
| 5 | Consultant speaks < 35% — excellent listening |
| 4 | Consultant speaks 35-45% — good balance |
| 3 | Consultant speaks 45-55% — acceptable but consultant-heavy |
| 2 | Consultant speaks 55-65% — too much talking |
| 1 | Consultant speaks > 65% — monologue, not a conversation |

**Analysis notes:**
- Measure per-phase, not just overall (discovery phase should be heavily prospect-led)
- Long consultant monologues (>60 seconds uninterrupted) are a red flag regardless of ratio
- Brief acknowledgments ("right", "I see", "absolutely") count as active listening, not talk time

---

## 2. Question Quality

**What to measure:** Open vs closed questions, depth of follow-up, relevance to qualification.

| Score | Criteria |
|-------|----------|
| 5 | Mostly open questions, excellent follow-up chains (3+ deep), quantifies impact |
| 4 | Good mix of open questions, some follow-up depth, attempts to quantify |
| 3 | Mix of open/closed, shallow follow-up, some quantification |
| 2 | Mostly closed/leading questions, minimal follow-up, no quantification |
| 1 | Scripted yes/no questions, no follow-up, no discovery depth |

**Follow-up chain example:**
- Level 1: "What's your biggest challenge?" → "Manual data entry"
- Level 2: "How much time does that take?" → "About 4 hours per week per person"
- Level 3: "How many people are affected?" → "12 team members"
- Level 4: "So roughly 48 hours/week — what's the cost of that in terms of missed opportunities?"

---

## 3. Pain Point Discovery

**What to measure:** Number and quality of pain points identified, with business impact.

| Score | Criteria |
|-------|----------|
| 5 | 3+ pain points with quantified business impact and emotional resonance |
| 4 | 2-3 pain points, most quantified, clear business impact |
| 3 | 1-2 pain points identified but not deeply quantified |
| 2 | Pain points mentioned but surface-level, no quantification |
| 1 | No real pain points uncovered — stayed at feature/wish level |

**A well-qualified pain point has:**
- The problem itself ("manual invoice processing")
- Who it affects ("the finance team of 8")
- Quantified impact ("20 hours/week, $15K/month in errors")
- Emotional weight ("the team is frustrated, turnover is high")

---

## 4. BANT Completion

**What to measure:** Whether each BANT criterion was addressed and the depth of information gathered.

### Budget (0-5)
| Score | Criteria |
|-------|----------|
| 5 | Specific budget range confirmed, timing known, approver identified |
| 4 | Budget exists, approximate range discussed |
| 3 | Budget topic raised, vague answer accepted |
| 2 | Budget mentioned in passing, no detail |
| 1 | Budget not discussed at all |

### Authority (0-5)
| Score | Criteria |
|-------|----------|
| 5 | Decision maker identified by name, buying process mapped, champion confirmed |
| 4 | Decision maker identified, some process understanding |
| 3 | "Others involved" acknowledged, no specifics |
| 2 | Authority assumed, not validated |
| 1 | Authority not explored |

### Need (0-5)
| Score | Criteria |
|-------|----------|
| 5 | Need confirmed, prioritized, connected to business outcomes |
| 4 | Need confirmed and summarized back to prospect |
| 3 | Need discussed but not validated/summarized |
| 2 | Need assumed from AI Strategy report, not verified |
| 1 | No explicit need confirmation |

### Timeline (0-5)
| Score | Criteria |
|-------|----------|
| 5 | Specific deadline or driver identified, urgency confirmed |
| 4 | General timeline discussed with some urgency indicator |
| 3 | "Sometime this quarter" — vague but present |
| 2 | Timeline mentioned but no urgency |
| 1 | Timeline not discussed |

## 5. Objection Handling

**What to measure:** How effectively the consultant responds to prospect concerns, pushback, or hesitation.

| Score | Criteria |
|-------|----------|
| 5 | Every objection acknowledged, reframed with evidence, and resolved. Uses frameworks (Feel/Felt/Found, acknowledge/reframe/quantify) |
| 4 | Most objections handled well, good technique on at least one |
| 3 | Objections addressed but with generic responses, some deflection |
| 2 | Objections acknowledged but not resolved, or deflected |
| 1 | Objections ignored, talked over, or triggered defensiveness |

**Common objections to watch for:**
- "We're not ready yet" → Timeline/urgency reframe
- "It's too expensive" → ROI/cost-of-inaction reframe
- "We've tried AI before" → Differentiation + case study
- "I need to talk to my team" → Offer to include them in next call
- "Just send me some info" → Stall — needs redirect to specific next step

---

## 6. Personalization & Preparation

**What to measure:** References to prospect's specific context from the AI Strategy report, their website, industry knowledge.

| Score | Criteria |
|-------|----------|
| 5 | 4+ specific references to prospect's context, industry expertise shown |
| 4 | 3 specific references, good industry awareness |
| 3 | 1-2 references to their context |
| 2 | Generic references that could apply to anyone |
| 1 | No personalization — clearly didn't prepare |

---

## 7. Next Step Quality

**What to measure:** Specificity and commitment level of the agreed next action.

| Score | Criteria |
|-------|----------|
| 5 | Specific meeting booked with decision maker, calendar invite sent, clear agenda |
| 4 | Specific next step with date/time proposed, decision maker awareness |
| 3 | Next step agreed but vague timing ("next week sometime") |
| 2 | "I'll send you some info and we'll reconnect" — no commitment |
| 1 | No next step discussed, call ended without forward motion |

---

## 8. Sentiment & Engagement Indicators

**What to detect in the prospect's language and behavior:**

### Positive Signals
- Asking detailed questions about implementation
- Sharing internal information (org structure, budget cycles, competitors)
- Using "we" language ("how would we implement...")
- Expressing frustration with current state (indicates motivation)
- Agreeing to involve others / schedule follow-up

### Negative Signals
- Short, clipped responses
- Repeatedly saying "interesting" without engagement
- Deflecting specific questions
- Mentioning competitors positively
- Rushing to end the call
- "Just send me some information"

### Sentiment Shift Detection
Track how prospect engagement changes across the 5 call phases:
- **Warming:** Moves from guarded → open → enthusiastic
- **Cooling:** Moves from interested → polite → disengaged
- **Recovery:** Cools at objection, consultant recovers engagement
- **Flatline:** Low engagement throughout — qualification failure

## Composite Score Calculation

| Component | Weight | Max Score |
|-----------|--------|-----------|
| Talk-to-Listen Ratio | 10% | 5 |
| Question Quality | 15% | 5 |
| Pain Point Discovery | 15% | 5 |
| BANT Completion | 20% | 20 (4×5) |
| Objection Handling | 15% | 5 |
| Personalization | 10% | 5 |
| Next Step Quality | 15% | 5 |

**Final score = weighted sum, normalized to 0-100**

Formula: `((ratio×10 + questions×15 + pain×15 + bant×20/4 + objections×15 + personalization×10 + nextstep×15) / 5) × 100/100`
