# Coaching Frameworks & Feedback Templates

## Feedback Structure

Every call analysis should produce feedback in this structure:

### 1. Executive Summary (for managers — quick scan)
- **Overall Score:** X/100 (Rating)
- **Call Outcome:** Next step secured / Stalled / Lost
- **Top Strength:** One thing done well
- **Critical Gap:** One thing to fix immediately
- **Recommended Action:** Specific coaching intervention

### 2. Phase-by-Phase Breakdown (for consultants — detailed learning)

For each of the 5 call phases:
- **Score:** X/20
- **What you did well:** Specific quotes or moments from the call
- **What was missed:** Specific elements from the script that were skipped
- **How to improve:** Concrete, actionable coaching with example language

### 3. Technique Analysis (for skill development)

#### Talk-to-Listen Analysis
Report the ratio with a visual breakdown per phase. Flag any monologues >60 seconds.

#### Question Quality Map
List each question asked, classify as:
- Open/Closed
- Surface/Deep
- Leading/Neutral
- Followed up / Dead end

#### Objection Handling Review
For each objection raised:
- What the prospect said (exact quote)
- How the consultant responded
- What a better response would look like
- Which framework applies (Feel/Felt/Found, Acknowledge/Reframe/Quantify)

### 4. Sentiment Timeline
Track prospect engagement through the call:
- Opening mood
- Key inflection points (what caused shifts)
- Closing mood
- Overall trajectory: Warming / Cooling / Recovery / Flatline

### 5. Coaching Recommendations (prioritized)

Maximum 3 recommendations, ranked by impact:

**Format for each:**
```
Priority: [HIGH/MEDIUM/LOW]
Area: [Phase or Technique]
Issue: [What's happening]
Impact: [Why it matters]
Action: [Specific thing to practice]
Example: [Exact language to use]
Practice drill: [How to rehearse this]
```

## Common Coaching Scenarios

### Scenario: "The Monologuer"
**Pattern:** Consultant talks >55% of the time, especially in discovery phase.
**Root cause:** Nervousness, over-preparation on product, fear of silence.
**Coaching:**
- Practice the "3-second rule" — wait 3 seconds after prospect stops talking
- Replace statements with questions: "Our platform does X" → "How are you handling X today?"
- Set a timer during practice calls — if you've talked for 30 seconds, ask a question

### Scenario: "The Checkbox Qualifier"
**Pattern:** BANT questions asked as a checklist with no depth or follow-up.
**Root cause:** Treating qualification as data collection, not conversation.
**Coaching:**
- Each BANT question should spawn 2-3 follow-ups
- Practice turning BANT into stories: "Tell me about the last time budget was approved for something like this"
- Role-play with deliberate vague answers to force deeper probing

### Scenario: "The Objection Dodger"
**Pattern:** When prospect raises a concern, consultant changes subject or says "good question" and moves on.
**Root cause:** Lack of confidence in handling pushback, fear of losing the deal.
**Coaching:**
- Learn the Feel/Felt/Found framework and practice with 5 common objections
- Objections are BUYING signals — they mean the prospect is engaged enough to push back
- Practice: "I appreciate you raising that. Let me address it directly..."

### Scenario: "The No-Closer"
**Pattern:** Call ends with "I'll send you some info" instead of a concrete next step.
**Root cause:** Fear of rejection, not wanting to be "pushy."
**Coaching:**
- Always have the next step ready BEFORE the call
- Use assumptive close: "I'd like to schedule a deeper dive with your team. I have Thursday at 2pm or Friday at 10am — which works better?"
- If they stall: "I totally understand. What would need to happen for you to feel ready to take the next step?"

### Scenario: "The Generic Pitcher"
**Pattern:** Solution framing is the same regardless of what the prospect said in discovery.
**Root cause:** Not taking notes, relying on memorized pitch, not connecting dots.
**Coaching:**
- Take notes during discovery, use prospect's EXACT words in your pitch
- Structure: "You mentioned [their words]. Here's how we address that specifically..."
- Always include ONE relevant case study — prepare 3-4 for different industries/pain points

## Progress Tracking Metrics (for Manager Overview)

### Per-Consultant Trends
Track these over time (weekly/monthly):
- Average overall call score
- Score by phase (spider/radar chart)
- BANT completion rate
- Next-step conversion rate (calls that end with a booked follow-up)
- Talk-to-listen ratio trend
- Most common coaching recommendation (is the same issue repeating?)

### Team-Level Insights
- Average score by consultant (identify who needs help)
- Score distribution (are most calls clustered or spread?)
- Most common team-wide weakness (training opportunity)
- Best performer analysis (what are they doing differently?)
- Call-to-opportunity conversion correlation with call scores
