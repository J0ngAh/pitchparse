# PitchParse Discovery Call Script & Qualification Framework

## Call Structure (30 minutes)

### Phase 1: Opening & Rapport (0-3 min)
**Goal:** Build trust, set the agenda, confirm the prospect's context.

**Required elements:**
- Greet by name, confirm role/title
- Reference the AI Strategy report they received (show you've done homework)
- Set expectations: "In the next 30 minutes, I'd love to understand your current challenges and see if there's a fit. Sound good?"
- Confirm time availability

**Red flags if missed:**
- Jumping straight to product pitch
- No agenda setting
- Not referencing their prior engagement (the AI Strategy email)

---

### Phase 2: Discovery & Pain Points (3-15 min)
**Goal:** Uncover genuine business pain that PitchParse can solve.

**Required questions (adapt to conversation flow):**
1. "Tell me about your current workflow for [area they indicated in the AI Strategy widget]. What does that look like day to day?"
2. "What's the biggest bottleneck or frustration with how that works today?"
3. "How is that impacting the team / the business? Can you quantify that?"
4. "Have you tried to solve this before? What happened?"
5. "If you could wave a magic wand, what would the ideal state look like?"

**Techniques to use:**
- Active listening — mirror their language back
- Go deeper with "Tell me more about that" and "What happens when..."
- Quantify pain: time wasted, cost, error rate, missed revenue
- Take notes on specific pain points for later reframing

**Red flags if missed:**
- Surface-level questions only (yes/no questions)
- Not quantifying the pain
- Talking more than listening (consultant should be <40% talk time in this phase)
- Not connecting pain to business impact

---

### Phase 3: BANT Qualification (15-22 min)
**Goal:** Determine if this prospect is a real opportunity.

#### Budget
- "Do you have a budget allocated for this kind of initiative, or would this need to be approved?"
- "What range are you thinking for a solution like this?"
- Listen for: existing budget, budget cycle timing, who controls budget

#### Authority
- "Who else would need to be involved in a decision like this?"
- "What does your typical buying process look like?"
- Listen for: decision maker vs influencer vs champion, committee dynamics

#### Need
- Already uncovered in Phase 2. Summarize: "So if I'm hearing you right, the core need is [X], which is costing you [Y]. Is that accurate?"
- Confirm priority level: "Where does this sit on your priority list right now?"

#### Timeline
- "When would you ideally want something like this in place?"
- "Is there an event or deadline driving the timing?"
- Listen for: urgency signals, fiscal year pressure, competitive pressure

**Red flags if missed:**
- Skipping budget entirely (common with junior reps)
- Not identifying the actual decision maker
- Accepting vague answers ("we'll figure it out") without probing
- Not establishing urgency or timeline

---

### Phase 4: Solution Framing & Value Prop (22-26 min)
**Goal:** Connect PitchParse's capabilities to their specific pain.

**Required approach:**
- Do NOT give a generic product demo
- Frame around THEIR pain: "Based on what you've told me about [specific pain], here's how we'd approach that..."
- Use 1-2 relevant case studies or examples: "We worked with [similar company] who had [similar problem] and achieved [specific result]"
- Quantify potential ROI: "If we could cut that process from 4 hours to 20 minutes, what would that mean for your team?"

**Techniques:**
- Feel/Felt/Found for objections: "I understand how you feel. Other companies felt the same way. What they found was..."
- Future pacing: "Imagine 3 months from now, your team is..."
- Social proof with specifics, not generics

**Red flags if missed:**
- Generic product dump with no personalization
- No case studies or social proof
- Not connecting features to their stated pain
- Failing to handle objections (deflecting or ignoring them)

---

### Phase 5: Close & Next Steps (26-30 min)
**Goal:** Secure a concrete next step — NOT a vague "we'll be in touch."

**Required elements:**
- Summarize key points: "So we've covered [pain], [need], [potential solution]. Does that feel like a good summary?"
- Propose specific next step: "I'd love to set up a deeper technical session with [their decision maker] and our solutions team. How does [specific date/time] look?"
- Handle stalls: "What would need to happen for you to move forward with exploring this?"
- Confirm follow-up: "I'll send you a summary email today with [specific items discussed]"
- Thank them for their time

**Red flags if missed:**
- Ending with "I'll send you some info" (no commitment)
- Not proposing a specific next meeting
- Not including the decision maker in the next step
- No follow-up commitment with a date
- Letting the prospect end the call without a clear next action

## Key Performance Indicators (KPIs)

| KPI | Target | How to Measure |
|-----|--------|----------------|
| Talk-to-listen ratio | Consultant < 40% talk time | Word count / speaking time per speaker |
| Pain points uncovered | ≥ 2 specific, quantified pain points | Explicit mention with business impact |
| BANT completion | All 4 criteria addressed | Each criterion explicitly discussed |
| Objections handled | 100% addressed (not deflected) | Every objection gets a response |
| Next step secured | Specific date/time committed | Calendar invite or verbal commitment |
| Personalization | References prospect's context ≥ 3 times | Mentions from AI Strategy report or research |
| Call structure adherence | All 5 phases covered | Each phase has substantive content |
| Follow-up commitment | Specific deliverable + date promised | "I'll send X by Y" |

## Scoring Rubric

### Overall Call Score (0-100)

| Range | Rating | Meaning |
|-------|--------|---------|
| 90-100 | Exceptional | All phases executed, strong technique, clear next step secured |
| 75-89 | Good | Most phases covered, minor gaps, next step secured |
| 60-74 | Needs Improvement | Key phases missed or weak, next step unclear |
| 40-59 | Poor | Multiple phases missing, poor technique, no clear outcome |
| 0-39 | Critical | Call fundamentally failed to qualify or advance the opportunity |

### Per-Phase Scoring (0-20 each, 5 phases = 100 total)
- **Opening (0-20):** Rapport, agenda setting, context reference
- **Discovery (0-20):** Depth of questions, active listening, pain quantification
- **BANT (0-20):** All 4 criteria addressed, depth of information gathered
- **Solution Framing (0-20):** Personalization, case studies, objection handling
- **Close (0-20):** Next step specificity, decision maker involvement, follow-up commitment
